# Corporate Finance & Accounting
Apple became the first company to reach a $3 trillion market capitalization in June 2023.
The GAAP revenue recognition principle requires recognizing revenue when earned, not when cash is received.
Depreciation methods include straight-line, declining balance, and units-of-production.
The debt-to-equity ratio measures financial leverage and varies significantly by industry.
EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization) is a measure of operating performance.
Working capital equals current assets minus current liabilities and indicates short-term financial health.
The weighted average cost of capital (WACC) represents a firm's average cost of financing.
Share buybacks reduce outstanding shares and can increase earnings per share without improving profitability.
Off-balance-sheet financing includes operating leases and special purpose vehicles.
The Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 established new financial disclosure and audit requirements after Enron's collapse.
Goodwill impairment testing is required annually and when indicators suggest value decline.
Cash flow from operations is often considered more reliable than net income due to accrual manipulations.
The current ratio (current assets divided by current liabilities) measures liquidity.
Return on equity (ROE) measures profitability relative to shareholders' equity.
Asset turnover ratio indicates how efficiently a company uses assets to generate revenue.
Financial leverage amplifies both gains and losses for equity holders.
The DuPont analysis decomposes ROE into profit margin, asset turnover, and financial leverage.
Inventory valuation methods (FIFO, LIFO, weighted average) affect reported profits and taxes.
The accounts receivable turnover ratio measures how quickly a company collects from customers.
Capital expenditure decisions use discounted cash flow analysis and net present value calculations.

# Banking & Monetary Policy
The Federal Reserve System was established by the Federal Reserve Act of 1913.
Fractional reserve banking allows banks to lend deposits while maintaining a reserve fraction.
Central bank independence is associated with lower inflation rates in economic studies.
The Taylor Rule suggests setting interest rates based on inflation and output gaps.
Bank capital requirements under Basel III include Tier 1 and Tier 2 capital with minimum ratios.
Quantitative easing expands the money supply by central bank purchases of government and other securities.
The federal funds rate is the overnight lending rate between depository institutions.
Liquidity coverage ratio requires banks to hold high-quality liquid assets covering 30-day outflows.
Money market funds invest in short-term, low-risk securities and offer market-determined yields.
Central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) could provide direct central bank access to consumers.
Negative interest rates have been implemented in Japan, Switzerland, and the Eurozone.
The money multiplier effect describes how base money expansion creates larger changes in money supply.
Bank stress tests evaluate capital adequacy under adverse economic scenarios.
Real-time gross settlement systems process payments individually and immediately.
Letter of credit provides payment guarantee in international trade transactions.
The discount rate is the interest rate at which banks borrow from the central bank's discount window.
Forward guidance communicates future policy intentions to influence current expectations.
Shadow banking includes non-bank financial intermediaries like money market funds and hedge funds.
Bail-in mechanisms require creditors to absorb bank losses before taxpayer bailouts.
Correspondent banking enables banks to access financial services in jurisdictions where they lack presence.

# Insurance & Risk Management
The law of large numbers underlies insurance by allowing mortality and loss prediction.
Reinsurance transfers portions of risk from primary insurers to other insurance companies.
Catastrophe bonds transfer insurance risk to capital markets investors.
The combined ratio (loss ratio plus expense ratio) measures insurance underwriting profitability.
Life expectancy tables form the basis for life insurance premium calculations.
Insurance reserves must be sufficient to pay all expected future claims.
Adverse selection occurs when high-risk individuals disproportionately purchase insurance.
Moral hazard arises when insurance coverage reduces incentives to avoid losses.
Enterprise risk management integrates risk oversight across organizational silos.
Parametric insurance pays based on trigger events rather than actual losses.
Lloyd's of London operates as a marketplace bringing together underwriters and brokers.
Value-at-risk (VaR) estimates potential loss at a specified confidence level over a time period.
Insurance density (premiums per capita) varies from over $4,000 in developed markets to under $50 in developing markets.
The guaranty system protects policyholders when insurance companies fail.
Cyber insurance premiums have grown rapidly as data breaches and ransomware attacks increase.
The insurance cycle alternates between soft markets (low prices) and hard markets (high prices).
Credit default swaps transfer default risk without transferring the underlying obligation.
Terrorism Risk Insurance Act provides federal backstop for catastrophic terrorism losses.
Insurance-linked securities enable capital markets investment in insurance risk.
Parametric weather derivatives pay based on measured weather conditions like rainfall or temperature.

# Real Estate & Property
The Case-Shiller U.S. National Home Price Index reached record highs in 2022 before moderating.
Cap rate (net operating income divided by property value) is a key commercial real estate metric.
Real estate investment trusts (REITs) must distribute at least 90% of taxable income as dividends.
The 30-year fixed-rate mortgage is relatively unique to the United States among global markets.
Commercial real estate categories include office, retail, industrial, multifamily, and hospitality.
Loan-to-value ratio is a primary consideration in mortgage underwriting.
The 2008 financial crisis originated in subprime mortgage defaults and mortgage-backed securities.
Property taxes are a main source of local government revenue in the United States.
Net operating income equals rental income minus operating expenses excluding debt service.
Real estate crowdfunding platforms have democratized access to property investments.
The Internal Revenue Code Section 1031 allows tax-deferred like-kind property exchanges.
Ground lease structures separate land ownership from building ownership.
Opportunity zones offer tax incentives for investments in designated low-income communities.
The debt service coverage ratio measures rental income relative to mortgage payments.
PropTech encompasses technology applications in real estate transactions and management.
Affordable housing requires subsidies because market rents exceed low-income households' ability to pay.
Commercial mortgage-backed securities (CMBS) pool mortgages for securitization.
Environmental site assessments identify contamination risks before property transactions.
Zoning regulations control land use and affect property values and development potential.
Build-to-rent developments construct single-family homes specifically for rental.

# Private Equity & Venture Capital
Private equity assets under management exceeded $13 trillion globally in 2023.
The J-curve describes typical private equity fund returns: negative early years followed by positive later returns.
Venture capital investments concentrate heavily in technology, healthcare, and consumer sectors.
Unicorn startups (valued at $1 billion or more) numbered over 1,200 globally as of 2024.
The typical private equity fund has a 10-year life with investment period and harvesting period.
Carried interest compensation (typically 20% of profits) aligns manager and investor interests.
Management fees for private equity funds typically range from 1.5% to 2% of committed capital.
Series A, B, C funding rounds represent successive stages of venture capital investment.
Leveraged buyouts use significant debt to finance acquisitions, with target company assets as collateral.
Growth equity investments in mature companies represent a middle ground between venture and buyout.
Secondary markets provide liquidity for limited partners seeking to sell fund interests.
Co-investment opportunities allow limited partners to invest alongside funds in specific deals.
Venture capital follows a power law distribution where a small percentage of investments generate most returns.
PIPE (Private Investment in Public Equity) transactions provide capital to public companies outside public markets.
Dry powder (uncommitted capital) in private equity exceeded $3 trillion in 2023.
Special purpose acquisition companies (SPACs) peaked in 2021 before regulatory scrutiny increased.
Continuation vehicles allow private equity funds to hold assets beyond initial fund life.
Operational value creation has become more important as multiples expansion has slowed.
Venture debt provides loans to venture-backed companies, complementing equity financing.
Impact investing seeks both financial returns and measurable social or environmental benefits.

# Cryptocurrency & Blockchain
Bitcoin's genesis block was mined on January 3, 2009 by Satoshi Nakamoto.
Ethereum introduced smart contracts, enabling programmable transactions on blockchain.
Bitcoin's maximum supply is capped at 21 million coins by its protocol.
Proof of stake consensus validates transactions based on staked cryptocurrency rather than computational power.
The Lightning Network enables faster, cheaper Bitcoin transactions through payment channels.
Decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols exceeded $50 billion in total value locked at peak.
Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) use blockchain to establish uniqueness and ownership of digital assets.
Bitcoin halving events occur approximately every four years, reducing block rewards by 50%.
The Mt. Gox exchange collapse in 2014 resulted in loss of approximately 850,000 Bitcoin.
Stablecoins maintain value pegged to fiat currencies through reserves or algorithmic mechanisms.
The FTX exchange bankruptcy in November 2022 involved approximately $8 billion in missing customer funds.
Ethereum transitioned from proof of work to proof of stake in September 2022 ("The Merge").
Zero-knowledge proofs enable verification of information without revealing the information itself.
Layer 2 solutions like rollups improve blockchain scalability by processing transactions off-chain.
MEV (Maximal Extractable Value) allows validators to profit by ordering transactions strategically.
Wrapped tokens represent assets from one blockchain on another blockchain.
Liquidity pools enable decentralized exchange trading without traditional order books.
Cryptocurrency mining energy consumption has attracted environmental criticism.
Regulatory frameworks for cryptocurrencies vary significantly between jurisdictions.
Self-custody through hardware wallets provides security independent of third-party custodians.

# International Trade & Commerce
Global trade volume exceeds $25 trillion annually, representing approximately 25% of world GDP.
The World Trade Organization succeeded GATT in 1995 with 164 member states.
Incoterms define responsibility for shipping, insurance, and customs clearance in international trade.
Letters of credit provide payment security in international transactions.
Trade finance facilitates approximately 80% of global trade through various instruments.
Most favored nation status requires equal trade treatment for all WTO members with limited exceptions.
Free trade agreements reduce or eliminate tariffs and trade barriers between member countries.
Anti-dumping duties counteract selling goods below cost or home market prices.
Rules of origin determine the national source of goods for tariff purposes.
Export controls restrict transfer of sensitive technologies and goods to certain countries.
Trade deficits occur when imports exceed exports in value.
Customs valuation determines the taxable value of imported goods.
Certificate of origin documents verify the country where goods were manufactured.
Bonded warehouses defer duty payment until goods enter domestic commerce.
Foreign trade zones allow duty-free import of goods for processing or re-export.
Trade remedies include anti-dumping duties, countervailing duties, and safeguard measures.
Export credit agencies provide government-backed financing for exports.
Supply chain due diligence increasingly requires verification of labor and environmental standards.
Digital trade agreements address e-commerce, data flows, and technology standards.
Bilateral investment treaties protect foreign investments and provide dispute resolution mechanisms.

# Taxation & Fiscal Policy
The Laffer curve theorizes that tax revenue decreases beyond an optimal tax rate.
Transfer pricing rules govern transactions between related parties across jurisdictions.
Tax treaties prevent double taxation and address withholding rates between countries.
The OECD Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (BEPS) project addresses multinational tax avoidance.
Capital gains are generally taxed at lower rates than ordinary income in most jurisdictions.
Tax loss harvesting realizes losses to offset gains and reduce tax liability.
Estate taxes apply to wealth transfer at death, with exemptions varying by jurisdiction.
Value-added tax (VAT) is collected at each stage of production and distribution.
The statutory tax rate differs from effective tax rates due to deductions and credits.
Qualified dividends receive preferential tax treatment in the United States.
Section 199A allows pass-through business income deductions of up to 20%.
Research and development tax credits incentivize innovation spending.
The alternative minimum tax (AMT) ensures minimum tax payment regardless of deductions.
Tax inversions relocate corporate headquarters to lower-tax jurisdictions.
Opportunity zone investments qualify for capital gains tax deferral and reduction.
1099 forms report various types of income to the Internal Revenue Service.
The standard deduction versus itemized deduction choice affects taxable income.
Estimated quarterly tax payments are required for income lacking withholding.
Net investment income tax adds 3.8% to investment income above thresholds.
Corporate tax rates vary globally from approximately 0% to over 35%.
